English for Graphic Design Professionals

A 3-year duration

From intermediate to advanced/professional levels, approximatively 3,120 hours total at 20 hours/week, you now have the opportunity to develop a comprehensive, immersive professional English curriculum tailored to the world of graphic design.

  • Learn More

This program fully integrates:

Deep language learning

This program guides learners from B1 to C2 levels, focusing on fluency, accuracy, and confidence in English. Through contextualized lessons, students master grammar, vocabulary, and communication strategies relevant to design, ensuring professional command of English in real-life creative scenarios, presentations, collaborations, and written communications with international clients or teams.

Industry-standard design communication

Students gain practical skills in visual and verbal communication for the design industry. The curriculum covers project pitching, creative briefs, client interactions, critiques, documentation, and cross-platform messaging. Emphasis is placed on clarity, persuasion, and creative expression—equipping learners to communicate effectively across design teams, clients, and global audiences using professional and industry-relevant English.

Real-world projects and internships

The course integrates client-based simulations, collaborative assignments, and internship opportunities with real companies. Learners apply language and design skills to authentic challenges—creating branding materials, design proposals, user interfaces, and marketing content. This experiential learning bridges the gap between classroom and industry, reinforcing English fluency through applied practice in real working contexts.

Certification and portfolio development

Upon completion, students receive an official certification and a professionally curated design-English portfolio. This includes written and oral presentations, client documents, and project outcomes. The portfolio showcases language mastery alongside creative competencies, serving as a powerful tool for job applications, freelancing, or further studies in international design or communication fields.

Year 1 – Foundational English for Creatives

Semester 1: Building the Language of Design

This semester establishes the foundation of English for visual thinkers. Students develop essential grammar and vocabulary while exploring the core principles of design language. Through creative exercises, discussions, and written tasks, they learn to describe color, layout, typography, and visual ideas with confidence. Special focus is given to verbal expression and basic writing relevant to design contexts.

Semester 1 Modules

  • 1. General English for Creative Professionals
  • 2. Design Vocabulary Bootcamp
  • 3. Grammar for Creatives
  • 4. Visual Storytelling & Moodboard Descriptions
  • 5. Writing Basics

Semester 2: Express, Critique, and Connect

In this semester, learners shift from comprehension to expression—refining their ability to speak, critique, and interact professionally in English. They explore interface and software language, develop their design voice, and begin working on critical and historical perspectives. The semester culminates in a final project where they present a design of their choice in fluent, polished English.

  • 10. Final Project: Describe & present your favorite project in English

Year 2 – Professional Communication in Design

Semester 1: Design Briefs, Clients & Team Communication

This semester focuses on mastering English in the core areas of professional design workflows. Students learn how to interpret briefs, respond to client emails, collaborate effectively within teams using Agile methods, and deliver compelling presentations. Emphasis is placed on clarity, tone, and the nuances of workplace interaction in English.

Semester 2: Creative Language, Brand Voice & Public Speaking

This semester hones creative expression and persuasive communication. Students explore branding language, sharpen their copywriting skills, and develop strategies for presenting themselves and their work. The final project simulates a real-world client pitch, integrating language, design, and public speaking under professional conditions.

Year 3 – Specialization, Portfolio & Real-World Experience

Semester 1: Designer Identity, Portfolio & Industry Engagement

This semester guides students in building a professional identity as English-speaking designers. Focus is placed on storytelling through portfolio work, mastering platforms like LinkedIn and Behance, and writing persuasive UX documentation. Students also explore industry trends, enhancing their fluency with real-world design discourse.

Semester 2: Real-World Simulation & Professional Launch

In this final semester, students step into real-world scenarios: internships or freelance simulations, client communication, job applications, and discussing social impact in design. The capstone project integrates all skills acquired over the 3 years, readying students for a global creative market.
