Semester 1

Module 1: General English for Creative Professionals

Weeks 1–4 | Semester 1, Year 1

Welcome to your first step in using English confidently as a creative professional! In this module, you’ll practice the essential language skills you need to talk about your work, your projects, and your creative journey. Each week focuses on real topics from your everyday life as a designer—from who you are and what inspires you, to the tools you use and your goals for the future. In the first week, you’ll learn how to introduce yourself and describe your creative identity. You’ll talk about what kind of design you do, what inspires you, and how you see yourself as a visual communicator. In week two, you’ll describe your daily creative life: the tools you use, your workspace, and the tasks you do in a typical day. You’ll also learn how to ask and answer questions about creative routines.

Week three will take you into the past—you’ll practice telling stories about past design experiences, projects, and moments that shaped you as a creative. Finally, in week four, we’ll look toward the future. You’ll learn how to describe your goals, talk about projects you’re planning, and give a short pitch about a future idea. By the end of this module, you’ll feel more confident speaking in class, writing about your work, and using design vocabulary in real conversations. You’ll complete creative tasks, speak with classmates, and even do a mini pitch. Let’s build your creative English together—step by step!

Week 1: Welcome & Creative Identity

This week is all about getting to know each other and feeling confident speaking in English about who you are as a designer. You’ll learn how to introduce yourself, talk about your design background, and describe your personal creative style. Through fun activities like visual boards and interviews, you’ll start building your English voice in the design world.

Week 2: Everyday Creative Life

Let’s talk about your daily design routine! This week, you’ll learn how to describe your typical workday, the tools you use, and how you collaborate with others. You’ll practice useful words and phrases to explain your creative process and improve your speaking through group conversations and class discussions.

Week 3: Creative History & Experience

It’s time to tell your story. You’ll learn how to speak and write about your past projects, inspirations, and learning experiences. We’ll focus on how to clearly explain what you’ve done before, using the past tense and time expressions. You’ll build a personal timeline and present a creative experience that matters to you.

Week 4: Planning & Projects

What are you working on next? What are your dreams as a designer? This week, you’ll learn how to talk about future plans and design goals using future tenses. You’ll create a design pitch for an upcoming (real or imagined) project, and get comfortable using English to share your ideas with others.

Assessment Components

At the end of this module, you’ll have the chance to show what you’ve learned in a creative and practical way. Don’t worry—these assessments are designed to help you feel more confident using English in real design situations!

    Speaking Quiz Presentation

Speaking: Portfolio Interview

You’ll take part in a short, friendly interview where you talk about your past experiences and future goals as a designer. This is your chance to show your English skills in a real conversation format, just like a job or internship interview.

Grammar & Vocabulary Quiz

You’ll complete a short quiz to review the grammar and design-related vocabulary we’ve practiced over the past four weeks. It will include useful words, verbs, and sentence structures to make sure you’re ready to use them in real life.

Presentation: Future Project Pitch

You’ll prepare and deliver a mini presentation about a future design project—real or imaginary. You can use slides, sketches, or moodboards to support your pitch. This is a great opportunity to practice sharing your ideas in English and using future tenses in a creative context.

Classes Breakdown

Class 1: Icebreaker & Designer Identity

  • Duration: 4 hours
  • Theme : "Introducing Yourself as a Designer in English"

Learning Objectives

  • Use the Present Simple and verb “to be” in personal introductions.
  • Describe your creative role, background, and interests clearly in English.
  • Build confidence and classroom community through icebreakers and pair work.

Here is a detailed breakdown of Class 1: Icebreaker & Designer Identity to guide your learning and enrich your experience. This includes timing, materials, instructions, and language objectives for your full 4-hour session.

Class Flow & Activities

Activity:

  • “Find Someone Who…”
  • – Creative Edition
  • Purpose: Build comfort speaking with peers using simple Present forms..

Find Someone Who…

  • …uses Figma for most of their work
  • …has designed a logo before
  • …loves minimalist design
  • …has taken a photography class
  • …prefers working on print projects
  • …uses Adobe Illustrator regularly
  • …has created a poster for an event
  • …is interested in UX/UI design
  • …follows design trends on Instagram or Behance
  • …has freelanced as a designer
  • …can explain what a grid system is
  • …has created a moodboard before
  • …loves using bold colors
  • …enjoys hand lettering or calligraphy
  • …has worked with clients in English

Find Someone Who…

  • …has made a portfolio website
  • …has designed for mobile apps
  • …uses Canva regularly
  • …has used stock photography in a project
  • …knows the Pantone Color of the Year
  • …has studied design abroad
  • …likes retro or vintage styles
  • …has created a presentation deck in English
  • …has watched a TED talk about creativity
  • …has participated in a design contest
  • …has presented a project in front of an audience
  • …uses Notion or Trello to organize work
  • …has received feedback in English on their designs
  • …has worked on a branding project
  • …can describe their design style in 3 words

Instructions:

  1. Distribute a worksheet: “Find someone who uses Figma / has designed a logo / loves minimalist design,” etc.
  2. Students mingle, asking each other questions (e.g., “Do you use Figma?”).
  3. Wrap up with a group discussion: “What did you learn about your classmates?”
